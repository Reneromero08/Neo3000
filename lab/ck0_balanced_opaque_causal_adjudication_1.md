## Causal process-object

### Carrier and borrowed state

The borrowed carrier is the exact immutable process-local opaque prefix root plus its bounded one-slot lease and child execution state. The root carries generic task semantics, the opaque vocabulary, strict response schemas, lifecycle law, and authorship boundary. It excludes the private binding, task/profile identity, internal candidate identity, expected resolution, support geometry, private scoring, and run keys.

This is a genuine Neo3000 prefix carrier with lawful child closure. It is not the classical CAT_CAS dirty tape because the substrate is not mutated and reversibly uncomputed byte-for-byte. The restored equivalence class is the exact carrier/root and terminal identity, admitted cache state, zero active leases, absence of branch state, cleanup, stable/candidate custody, historical evidence custody, free sidecar port, and absent run lock.

### Parent processes and relational state

Two branch requests produce model-authored diagnostic rankings over separately keyed branch-local extensional presentations. Those rankings do not become the transform parents. The controller uses the frozen public geometry and private binding to normalize two distinct five-member opaque parent relations. Each relation remains unresolved alone; together they have a singleton relational intersection.

The full-information transform receives both complete parent relations. A deletion condition still executes and privately verifies both branches, then replaces exactly one transform-visible relation with its four-field commitment-only receipt while preserving the other relation and parent order. This is the causal intervention point.

### Transform, extraction, and verification

The model authors the transform operator and opaque ranking. Extraction receives only that ranking and its run/stage-bound commitment, then the model authors one opaque selection. Only after selection does the controller apply the private correspondence, verify whether the selection matches the preregistered singleton, and compute the five-example public score. The model never receives that verification or score.

The process therefore separates:

- controller-authored geometry, private binding, commitments, intervention, verification, scoring, custody, and restoration;
- model-authored diagnostic branch rankings, relational transform, opaque transform ranking, extraction choice, and carrier acknowledgements.

## Causal findings

With both complete relations visible, the model-authored transform placed the private singleton first, extraction selected it, and controller scoring was 5/5. With parent-0 informative content withheld only at transform, the transform top and extraction did not reproduce the singleton and scoring was 3/5. The symmetric parent-1 deletion produced the same bounded failure pattern at 3/5. Restoration, cleanup, custody, and zero-lease closure passed in every condition.

This demonstrates, under the exact frozen encoding, that:

1. the complete pair of opaque parent relations was sufficient for the observed relational resolution;
2. removing either complete parent payload at the transform boundary changed the model-authored transform/extraction outcome;
3. both directional parent-information dependencies are supported for this carrier;
4. both parent relations were causally necessary only in the bounded operational sense of reproducing this full-information result under this exact encoding.

It does not establish abstract semantic necessity independent of payload shape, general two-parent necessity, model-internal discovery of the parent geometry, transfer, task advantage, or generalization.

## Quantitative null analysis

The two five-member supports have one shared member, so their union contains nine opaque alternatives.

Under a deliberately simple support-conditioned uniform-choice null:

- full-information singleton hit: `1/9`, approximately 11.1%;
- singleton hit after either deletion, conditional on choosing among the surviving five: `1/5`, or 20%;
- miss after either deletion: `4/5`, or 80%;
- two deletion misses: `(4/5)^2 = 16/25`, or 64%;
- one full hit plus both misses, additionally assuming independence: `(1/9)(4/5)(4/5) = 16/225`, approximately 7.1%.

This is not a formal p-value. Uniform exchangeability, support-conditioned choice, and independence are not established. The decoder is deterministic at temperature zero with fixed stage seeds; the three conditions share model, prompts, geometry, and one binding; extraction follows a ranking process; and the schema permits the full opaque vocabulary. The two deletion misses alone are common under the simple null. The full-information hit is the main relational signal.

A fresh private alias/presentation binding is the cleanest next invariance test because it changes the nuisance identity, support positions, presentation binding, and commitment values while preserving the mechanism. It directly probes lexical alias preference, support-order preference, presentation-label preference, and deterministic binding/seed interaction. It does not by itself eliminate commitment-receipt shape or establish seed robustness.
